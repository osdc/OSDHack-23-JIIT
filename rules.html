Rules

    1. The Hackathon is only open for students of JIIT.
    2. The participants should register before 9:00pm on 19th of April 2023.
    3. Participants may bring their laptops and other necessary equipment. However access to laboratory computers and unrestricted internet via RJ45 cables will be provided by the organizers.
      (NOTE: MacBook users need to bring their own port extenders to access internet via RJ45 cables).
    4. Each team should consist of at least 2 and at most 4 members.
    5. For the students of 2nd Year enrolled in the B.Tech (CSE&IT) branch it is compulsory to participate in OSDHack'23 as a part of their TA Evaluation of 10 out of 25 Marks in the CSE/IT course they are registered in.
    6. Participants are not allowed to make teams with members from outside the Institute. However JIIT-62 and JIIT-128 participants can make teams together.
    7. There is no restriction on the type of project you come up with, or the languages/tools you use. It can be anything from a web app to a device driver (as long as it is not a hardware project).
    8. All code and assets must be created and committed within the time constrictions of the Hackathon. Late commits may not be considered, so avoid any last minute Red Bull rushes.
    9. Reusing or incorporating existing open source code or copyright free assets to create your magic (because that's what someone else's code seems like) is allowed, provided that they are appropriately attributed and their license agreements are followed.
    10. Your project or the same underlying idea behind the project has not been knowingly submitted by you in any other hackathon/event before.
    11. All code written must be compatible with an Open-Source license and should be public.
    12. Git should be the primary version control system.
    13. Participants must submit their final project within the given time limits.
    14. Mid Evaluations will be done by the JIIT Faculty in addition to the OSDC Core Team based on the following criteria:
      • Mid-Eval 1: The evaluation will be based upon several parameters i.e. Idea Feasibility & Utility alongwith it's Importance & Relevance for the user.
      • Mid-Eval 2: The evaluation will be based upon percentage of completion of the project alongside plagiarism check will be done
    15. Any kind of misbehavior or violation of the rules can lead to disqualification from the competition.
    16. The Organizing Team's decision on conflicts, results, prizes or any other dispute would be made in all fairness and stands final.

FAQs

What's a Hackathon, and can I participate?
expand_more
A Hackathon is an intense event that brings together designers and developers along with industry experts to identify problems and create software solutions, usually within 24-48 hours. Whether that's high school, undergrad, or graduate, we believe you can get something out of the event. Only students of JIIT are allowed to participate in the offline OSDHack.
Can participants use the equipment provided by the organizers in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
No, participants must bring their own laptops and other necessary equipment. Internet connection will be provided by the organizers.
Is registration required to participate in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Yes, participants are required to register for the event prior to the deadline.
Can participants make teams with members from outside the college in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
No, participants are not allowed to make teams with members from outside the college. However JIIT-62 and JIIT-128 participants can make teams together.
How many members can be in a team in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Each team should consist of at least 2 and at most 4 members. Pro Tip: Aim to have a mix of people with both design and developer skills.
What if I don't have a team or an idea?
expand_more
Don't worry, most people don’t! We’ll have team formation and ideation events geared towards helping you find people to work with. It’s perfectly normal to go around to people, tell them you’re looking for a team, and ask if you can help out. You can also show yourself off at #find-teammates! (In the discord server)
Can participants use copyrighted materials in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
No, participants must not use any copyrighted materials.
How should participants submit their final project in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Participants must submit their final project within the given time limits.
What happens if a participant violates the rules or engages in misbehavior in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Any kind of misbehavior or violation of the rules can lead to disqualification from the competition.
